Bicycle Crunches

When doing bicycle crunches, engage your core muscles, open the elbows and keep your neck relaxed. Breathe out as you crunch, keep your shoulder blades off the mat and maintain a steady rhythm throughout the entire exercise.

3 sets of 12 to 20 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Lie on your back, lift your shoulders off the mat and raise both legs.
2. Bring one knee and the opposing elbow close to each other by crunching to one side, and fully extend the other leg.
3. Return to the starting position and then crunch to the opposite side. Repeat until the set is complete.
Benefits: Adding bicycle crunches to your workout routine helps to increase core strength, slims down your waist, and improves your stability and flexibility. This exercise works your abs and obliques and because it keeps your body moving constantly, boosts heart rate, and burns calories.
